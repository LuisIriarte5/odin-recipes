Thanksgiving Turkey

Description

It is thanksgiving season, and we have a special recipe to help you impress your guests. Roasting a thanksgiving turkey is a special occasion, so you must take your time, be prepared to invest around 3 hours and 30 minutes in this flavorful journey. Your effors will result in the most delicious dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole turkey, neck and giblets removed
  • Aluminium foil
  • Kitchen string
  • 1 bottle of champagne
  • 1 can chicken broth
  • To season the turkey:
    • 2 tablespoons dried parsley
    • 2 tablespoons ground dried rosemary
    • 2 tablespoons rubbed dried sage
    • 2 tablespoons dried thyme leaves
    • 1 tablespoon lemon-pepper seasoning
    • 1 tablespoon salt
    • 2 stalks celery cut into slices
    • 1 medium orange cut in 8 wedges
    • 1 medium onion chopped into large pieces
    • 1 medium carrot cut into slices

Preparation

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C)
  2. Line a roaster with sheets of aluminium foil long enough to wrap around turkey
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the parsley, rosemary, sage, thyme, lemon-pepper seasoning, and salt.
  4. Rub the seasoning mixture inside the turkey cavity.
  5. Stuff the turkey with the orange wedges, onion, carrot, and celery.
  6. Tie the turkey legs with kitchen string, then tuck the wings under the body.
  7. Place the turkey on the foil in the roasting pan.
  8. Pour champagne and chicken broth over the turkey, ensuring some liquid gets into the cavity.
  9. Bring the aluminium foil up and over the top of turkey, sealing it.
  10. Roast the turkey in the preheated oven until juices run clear for over 2 1/2 to 3 hours.
  11. Uncover the turkey and continue baking until the skin turns golden brown for an additional 30 to 60 more minutes. If you have a thermometer, insert it into the thickest part of thigh and it should read 180 degrees F (82 degrees C)
  12. Remove the turkey from the oven, cover it with two sheets of aluminium foil, and allow it to rest in a warm area for 10 to 15 minutes before slicing.
